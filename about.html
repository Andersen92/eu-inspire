European Union - Kyrgyz Republic Cooperation for Development

The European Union (EU) and the Kyrgyz Republic have been partners since the country’s independence in 1991. The EU cooperation activities in Kyrgyzstan include bilateral, regional and thematic programmes, which are guided by the Partnership and Cooperation Agreement between the EU and Kyrgyzstan, the EU-Central Asia Strategy and the EU Strategy on Connecting Europe and Asia. The new Enhanced Partnership and Cooperation Agreement is expected to enter into force soon.

The Multiannual Indicative Programme for Kyrgyzstan defines three priority areas for EU support to Kyrgyzstan in 2021-2027 with up to EUR 62 million in grants allocated for 2021-2024:

Priority areas of Multiannual
Indicative Programme 2021-2027

Governance and digital transformation

  • Promote rule of law
  • Improve governance
  • Build an effective & non-discriminatory digitalisation

Human development

  • Develop inclusive and equitable quality education and improve digital, media and green skills
  • Gender equality, youth empowerment & human rights

Green and climate-resilient economy

  • Support climate and environmental action and promote Integrated Water Resource Management
  • Support the development of green business

The bilateral programme is complemented by additional resources to support regional cooperation and priority thematic issues, such as the EU Investment Facility, Erasmus+ Higher Education Programme, resilience to COVID-19, trade development, and security and scientific cooperation.

Cross-cutting priorities, such as human rights, gender equality, youth and climate change, and fundamental principles, including leaving no one behind and the need to build back better, are central to the EU’s cooperation.

The EU support to Kyrgyzstan takes many forms, including:

  • Budget support: direct financial transfers to the state, conditional on engaging in sustainable development reforms,
  • Bilateral & regional grants to implementing partners,
  • Blending: using grants to complement loans to attract public and private investors, especially for infrastructure projects and support to the private sector, and
  • Twinning: facilitating partnerships & sharing expertise between Kyrgyzstan and the EU member states.

To maximise the impact of the cooperation in Kyrgyzstan, where possible, the EU works closely with its member states and partners in a Team Europe approach. The priority areas and EU’s regional and thematic work align with the UN’s 2030 Agenda, the Paris Agreement and the EU’s Global Gateway Strategy, promoting sustainable partnerships for a connected world.

Success stories of EU
support to the Kyrgyz Republic

Building on the EU’s support to the Kyrgyz educational sector, in 2022, the EU committed EUR 32 million via budget support and technical assistance to the state to improve the educational quality, promote gender equality and multilingual education, and help the government implement its Education Development Strategy 2040.With the help of the EU:

  • all new textbooks, learning and teaching materialsare madegender responsive,
  • pre-service teacher training will include multilingual education programmesa cross the country,
  • media and green skills are integrated into compulsory school education.

HIGHLIGHT
Civil Society Organisations (CSOs)

The EU is committed to strengthening the Kyrgyz civil society’s ability to reach, empower and represent local populations and protect their rights.

By supporting CSOs as actors of governance, the EU promotes sustainable growth and social service delivery, as well as human rights, gender equality, youth empowerment, and fundamental freedoms in general. The EU's support to the Kyrgyz CSOs has contributed to tackling social discrimination, promoting freedom of expression, advancing digital skills among youth, women and local communities, and fostering socio-economic development in the regions.

The EU’s engagement with CSOs is guided by the "EU Roadmap for Engagement with Civil Society in the Kyrgyz Republic 2021-2027”, “EU Human Rights and Democracy Country Strategy for the Kyrgyz Republic 2021-2024", and the EU Communication “The roots of democracy and sustainable development: Europe's engagement with Civil Society in external relations”.

HIGHLIGHT II
Erasmus+

Erasmus+, the EU’s programme for education, training, youth and sports, offers a range of opportunities to higher education students, staff and institutions in Kyrgyzstan.

Between 2015 and 2020, Erasmus+:

  • Supported mobility of up to one year between Kyrgyzstan and the EU for over 1100 students, researchers and staff,allowing both sides to learn from each other academically and professionally.
  • Awarded over 75 Erasmus Mundus scholarships to Master’s students from Kyrgyzstan, covering the tuition, traveland living allowance to obtain a double or multiple degree from at least two different European countries.
  • Contributed to modernising higher education institutions in Kyrgyzstan. For instance, Kyrgyz institutions benefitted from around 80 occasions under the Erasmus+ Capacity Building in Higher Education action.

Through Erasmus+, the EU aims to further promote mobility and enhance higher education in Kyrgyzstan in 2021-2027.
