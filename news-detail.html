Digital skills trainings for local trainers / project “DigiKonush – Building a transparent future in rural communities of Kyrgyzstan via digitalization”

From April 19 to April 27, 2022, trainings of the project “DigiKonush – Building a transparent future in rural communities of Kyrgyzstan via digitalization” were held in Osh and Issyk-Kul regions, within the framework of which at least 30 local teachers of universities, schools, vocational schools, as well as activists learned how to develop their own e-learning courses. The event was also attended by residents of the Batken region.

The trainings were conducted by experts Talant Sultanov, Aziz Soltobaev and Fariza Chilmergenova at the Center of Digital Skills at Osh State University and Issyk-Kul State University named after Kasym Tynystanov. The same Center was previously opened at Batken State University. More than 50,000 euros were spent to equip these Centers of Digital Skills within the framework of the project.

These trainings are built in a gamesome, relaxed format, but at the same time very intense in content. Future trainers learned how to promote and monetize their product, studied the market and competition, and learned how to identify users of their services. The advantage of these trainings was that they were held in the Kyrgyz language, which is not common in the field of ICT and is very valuable for representatives of the regions, in particular for rural residents. In addition, the participants highly appreciated the demand for such trainings in connection with the digitalization and creative economy policy promoted both in the country and in the world.

At the end of the training, participants acquired the necessary and sufficient knowledge to create courses in various areas of digital skills, ranging from computer literacy courses to graphic design for seamstresses.

The project “DigiKonush – Building a transparent future in rural communities of Kyrgyzstan through digitalization” (2020-2023) is implemented by the Center for Civic Initiatives “Leader” in partnership with the Estonian Center for International Development with the support of the European Union and is aimed at increasing the digital literacy of women and youth, reducing the digital divide between urban and rural populations.
